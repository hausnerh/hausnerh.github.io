About

I am a neutrino experimentalist interested in 4‑flavor oscillation physics and physics beyond the Standard Model with accelerator neutrinos. I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at Fermilab, where I work on ICARUS; contributing to the singlen‑detector muon‑neutrino disappearance search and helping prepare the for multi‑detector Short‑Baseline Neutrino program's future measurement, hoping to put definitive limits on oscillations at the eV2‑scale mass‑squared splitting.

I received my PhD from the University of Wisconsin–Madison in Brian Rebel's group, where I implemented a covariance‑matrix fitting technique for sterile‑neutrino searches at NOvA. Outside of analysis, I care about building community in neutrino physics—as a SULI mentor, and through SBN Young, a grassroots group of early-career researchers in the SBN program.
